Hello! I’m Sarah.

I’m a graphic designer & web developer.
I like riding my motorcycle, travelling the world, and learning new things. In my spare time, I make things that are beautiful to look at & easy to use.

For the last eight years, I’ve been running a full-time freelance design business. I love my work and I love my clients, but I’ve realized it’s time for a change. Building and growing a business has been an incredible experience. It’s taught me to communicate better, to back up my design decisions with reasoned arguments, and to manage my time effectively.

It’s also taught me that running a business is not where I excel. I want to spend more time doing work I love (design!) and less time doing work I don’t (accounting, responding to RFPs, using words like “synergistic.”)

I am looking for a chance to do better work.

As a designer-developer and a self-styled “jill of all trades,” I’ve had the opportunity to learn all kinds of skills. If your copy doesn’t feel quite right, I can help you tweak it. Need someone to manage a project, keep everything on track, and oversee everyone? I’m your girl. I’m also very strong technically. Developers love me because I use git with gusto and I’m not afraid to get my hands messy with code.

Over the last three years, I’ve visited over thirty-four different countries in four different continents. I’ve spent a total of sixteen months in Canada, all while running a successful, thriving business. Working while on the move hasn’t always been easy, but it’s taught me to balance my workload and communicate effectively. As a result, I’m efficient, coordinated, and I’m an absolute champion remote worker.

It’s my exploratory nature that makes me a great designer. I am always looking, always learning, always seeking new things. As a result, my work is varied, considered, and ever-improving.

In short: I am great. Hire me!

Here's a little more about me

I'm a self-starter

Did I mention that whole "running a business for eight years" thing? I am super motivated.

I'm detail-oriented

I've yet to meet a pixel I didn't think need nudging down a squidge, or a line of code I thought couldn't be shorter, or a colour I thought didn't need to be tweaked. But I never let perfect be the enemy of great. Instead, I'll progressively enhance a design until it's better and better.

Running a business means you've got to have a lot of complimentary skills. I've done copywriting, project management, technical writing, documentation, accounting, marketing, on top of my usual designer-developer things. I solve problems

Any time something comes up that throws me for a loop, I try to figure out a way to work around it, so that I can be more efficient and effective.

I'm quick like a bunny

Time management is one of those things you either pick up or die trying running a business. I'm used to juggling six different projects at a time, each with different deadlines, requirements, and clients in different timezones. On top of that, I've often been in the jungles of South America or wandering the deltas of Southeast Asia while I've been doing all that. In short: I get stuff done.

Custom WordPress site for a spa

Cascades Day Spa is one of Halifax’s longest-running and most well-known day spas. They have a loyal clientele and an excellent reputation, but their old website just wasn’t pulling its weight anymore. Built on a Contribute platform, it was difficult to update, hard to navigate, and its design was sorely outdated. The site needed an overhaul to be in line with their business and to allow for continued growth and new customers.

They requested that the site be simple, clean, and easy to navigate. At the same time, they knew they needed something that would be accessible on phones and tablets. Of primary importance were the gift certificates and the book online functionality, both of which needed to be as easy to use as possible from both a client and a company perspective.

After evaluating their precise needs from an administrative point of view, I suggested a few options for online gift certificates, set up an online appointment booking form, and set up a custom WordPress installation. Promotions are managed as a special post type, allowing for easy entry without replicating content in multiple places.
